// Comparison

MARAPONE
vs DOCUMENT CRUNCH.

Document Crunch is a focused tool — contract review and risk identification done well. Marapone is a multi-module private build that includes contract review and a lot more. Different shape; different price; not always either/or.

// TL;DR

THREE BULLETS

When Document Crunch is best

Contract review is your single biggest pain, you want a SaaS subscription you can switch on tomorrow, and the existing playbook library is enough.

When Marapone is best

Contract review is one of several workflows you need, you want a private system on your infrastructure, and you'd rather pay once than per-seat.

The honest middle

If contract review is the only thing you need and you have under five users, Document Crunch is faster to value. Marapone wins as the scope broadens.

When Document Crunch is the better fit

Stick with Document Crunch if…

  • • Contract review is the single biggest blocker on your team's time.
  • • You have a small number of users and SaaS pricing pencils out.
  • • Their pre-built risk playbooks already match what you'd flag manually.
  • • You want to be live this week, not in two weeks.

When Marapone is the better fit

Pick Marapone if…

  • • Contract review is one of several workflows you need automated.
  • • You want the rules tuned to your specific risk register, not a generic library.
  • • You'd rather own the system than rent it.
  • • Per-seat pricing across 30+ users no longer makes sense.
