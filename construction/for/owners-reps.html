// The four things the GC isn't telling the owner

WHAT YOUR JOB IS REALLY ABOUT

PAIN 01

GC daily logs are thin and late

The logs that arrive Tuesday for last Wednesday say "rain in AM, work continued." No crew counts, no blockers, no record of the consultant visit you know happened. You can't audit what isn't written.

→ FIX: Daily Log Synthesizer (review-side) cross-reads what the GC submitted against site photos, RFI traffic, and email to surface what's missing or contradicts the record.

PAIN 02

Change orders arrive without paper trail

A $140k CO lands in your inbox. The GC says it was directed verbally at the OAC three weeks ago. The minutes say nothing. Now you're the one telling the owner whether to approve or fight it.

→ FIX: Change Order Risk Tracker continuously scans email, RFIs, and meeting minutes for scope drift and tells you whether the proof actually exists.

PAIN 03

Deficiencies live in three different lists

The GC's punch app, the consultant's PDF, your own walkthrough notes. None of them match. The owner asks for a single answer on completion percentage and you have to spend a day reconciling before you can speak.

→ FIX: Deficiency List AI consolidates every source — GC, consultant, your own notes — into one ranked list with status and re-checks itself as items close.

PAIN 04

Monthly status reports take three days

By the time you've stitched together the schedule, the cost report, the RFI log, and the deficiency status into something the owner can read in fifteen minutes, the data is already a week stale.

→ FIX: The build assembles a defensible monthly status pack on demand, drawing from every source you already receive — so it's hours, not days.
