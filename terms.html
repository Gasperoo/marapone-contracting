TERMS OF SERVICE

These are the terms under which Marapone Contracting Inc. provides services. We've written them in plain language. If something is unclear, email us — we'd rather explain it than have a dispute about it later.

Effective date: January 1, 2026  ·  Last updated: March 2026  ·  Marapone Contracting Inc. (Canada)

01 — Parties

WHO THESE TERMS APPLY TO

"Marapone," "we," "us," or "our" refers to Marapone Contracting Inc., a company incorporated in Canada, operating from Canada and Italy.

"Client," "you," or "your" refers to the business or individual who engages Marapone for services by agreeing to a project scope and making payment.

By engaging our services — whether through a signed proposal, email confirmation, or initial deposit payment — you agree to these Terms of Service in full.

02 — Services

WHAT WE PROVIDE

Marapone builds private, locally-deployed AI systems for construction companies, logistics and trade businesses, and marketing teams. Services include but are not limited to:

  • —Custom AI model fine-tuning on client-provided document sets
  • —Blueprint auditing, RFI intelligence, and daily log synthesis systems
  • —Trade document processing, freight invoice auditing, and compliance risk systems
  • —AI-powered marketing content, campaign builds, and brand voice systems
  • —Local application deployment, installation, and team training
  • —Post-delivery support plans (optional, as described on our pricing page)

The exact scope of each engagement is defined in a written project proposal or scoping document agreed upon before work begins. No work outside the defined scope will be carried out without a separate written agreement and pricing confirmation.

03 — Engagement

HOW AN ENGAGEMENT STARTS

All engagements begin with a discovery call or free assessment, during which we evaluate your document types, infrastructure requirements, and project scope. Following this, we issue a written proposal outlining deliverables, timeline, and total price.

An engagement is formally confirmed when the client:

  • —Accepts the written proposal in writing (email confirmation is sufficient), and
  • —Pays the required deposit as outlined in Section 4

We take one client at a time. Confirmation of your engagement secures your place in the build queue. Until the deposit is received, we cannot guarantee availability.

04 — Payment

PAYMENT TERMS & DEPOSITS

A deposit of 10%–50% of the total project fee is required before any build work begins. The remainder is due upon final delivery and handover of all deliverables.

Deposit percentage: The required deposit varies based on the scale, complexity, and hardware requirements of the project. Smaller or lower-complexity builds (e.g., Starter or Pilot tiers) typically require a deposit in the 10%–25% range. Larger or infrastructure-heavy builds (e.g., Full Build or Plus tiers involving custom server or GPU configurations) typically require a deposit in the 25%–50% range. The exact deposit amount will be stated clearly in your project proposal before you are asked to pay anything.

Hardware costs and the deposit: Marapone maintains its own hardware and software infrastructure for building, training, and testing AI systems on our end. However, if your project requires specific client-side hardware — such as dedicated GPU servers, workstations, networking equipment, or air-gapped machine configurations — those costs are handled as follows:

OPTION A

Client provides the hardware directly

If you already own or can procure the required hardware (servers, GPUs, workstations, etc.), you supply it and no hardware costs are added to your invoice. We deploy and configure the system on your equipment.

OPTION B

Marapone procures hardware on your behalf

If your project requires hardware that you do not already own and you would like us to source and configure it, the full cost of that hardware is calculated upfront, itemised in your proposal, and added to your deposit. For example: if your build requires a 3-GPU server, we will quote the specific components, calculate the total cost, and include that amount in your deposit. You own all hardware we procure for your build — it is delivered to you or deployed in your environment at project completion.

No surprise costs: All hardware requirements and associated costs are identified and disclosed in the project proposal before you commit. If unforeseen hardware needs arise during a build, we will notify you immediately, provide a written cost estimate, and will not proceed with any additional spend without your written approval.

Invoicing: Invoices are issued electronically. The deposit invoice is issued upon proposal acceptance. The final invoice is issued upon delivery of all deliverables. Payment is due within 14 days of invoice date unless otherwise agreed in writing.

Late payment: Invoices unpaid after 30 days from the due date may incur a 1.5% monthly interest charge on the outstanding balance. We will contact you before applying any late fees.

Accepted payment methods: We accept Visa, Mastercard, American Express, PayPal, Interac e-Transfer, business cheque, and bank wire transfer. All prices are in Canadian dollars (CAD) unless otherwise specified in writing.

05 — Deliverables

WHAT YOU RECEIVE

Upon completion of your build and receipt of the final payment, Marapone will deliver:

  • ✓Full source code for all custom software built for your project
  • ✓Fine-tuned AI model weights trained on your document sets
  • ✓Installation guide and technical documentation
  • ✓Any additional deliverables specified in the project proposal (e.g., walkthrough calls, training sessions)

You receive everything. We retain nothing. We will not keep copies of your source code, model weights, or any documents you provided for training. This is a complete handover, not a licence.

06 — Intellectual Property

WHO OWNS WHAT

Your deliverables: Upon full payment, all custom code, trained model weights, and associated documentation produced specifically for your project are assigned to you in full. You own them unconditionally. You may modify, distribute, sell, or repurpose them without restriction or further payment to Marapone.

Your documents: All documents, data, and materials you provide to Marapone for the purpose of model training or analysis remain your property at all times. We do not claim any rights to your business data.

Our pre-existing tools and methods: Any general tools, frameworks, internal processes, or methodologies that Marapone uses to build your system — and which were not created specifically for your project — remain Marapone's property. These are the underlying skills and infrastructure we bring to every build, not client-specific deliverables.

07 — Confidentiality

KEEPING YOUR INFORMATION PRIVATE

We treat all client information — business documents, project details, pricing, internal workflows, and communications — as confidential. We do not share, sell, publish, or disclose any client information to third parties without your express written consent, except where required by law.

Documents you provide for model training are used solely for the purpose of building your AI system. They are processed on our secure local hardware during development, not uploaded to cloud services or external APIs, and are deleted from our systems upon project delivery.

We will not reference you as a client in marketing materials or case studies without your explicit written permission.

If either party requires a formal Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA) prior to the discovery process, we are happy to execute one. Contact us to arrange this before sharing any sensitive materials.

08 — Guarantee & Warranty

WHAT WE STAND BEHIND

30-day results guarantee: If the delivered system does not save your team at least 10 hours of document review time in the first 30 days of active use, we will issue a full refund upon written request. No conditions, no investigation, no awkward process — just email us.

Post-delivery defect warranty: If the system fails due to an error in our build — a bug in our code, a broken module, output quality below what we demonstrated, or install failure following our own documented steps — we will fix it at no charge, with no timeline haggling.

What is not covered at no charge: Issues caused by changes you or your team made to the code, OS or dependency upgrades, hardware migration, or requests for new features after delivery are not covered under the defect warranty. These are quoted separately, transparently, and fairly.

We make no warranties — express or implied — that the AI system will catch 100% of all errors in every document. No AI system can. We warrant that it will perform at or above the level demonstrated during the assessment and final walkthrough.

09 — Cancellation

CANCELLATION & TERMINATION

Client cancellation before build begins: If you cancel after paying a deposit but before any substantive build work has started, we will refund the deposit in full minus any hardware that has already been procured on your behalf (which remains yours).

Client cancellation mid-build: If you cancel after build work has commenced, Marapone will invoice for work completed to date at a pro-rated rate based on project milestones. Any portion of the deposit not consumed by completed work will be refunded. Hardware already procured is yours to keep or return; it cannot be refunded by Marapone once ordered.

Marapone cancellation: We reserve the right to cancel an engagement if the client fails to provide necessary materials within a reasonable timeframe, fails to make payment, or behaves in a way that makes the engagement untenable. In this case, we will refund any portion of the deposit not yet consumed by completed work.
