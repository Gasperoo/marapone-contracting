FIRST CLIENT CLOSING CHECKLIST
Use this for every prospect who has moved from Assessment to active Close stage.
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

STEP 1 — ASSESSMENT DELIVERED
[ ] Documents received from prospect
[ ] Acknowledgement sent within 2 hours: "Received — findings within 24 hours."
[ ] AI analysis completed on their actual documents
[ ] Written findings prepared — specific, referenced to their documents, non-generic
[ ] Findings include: what was found, what a full system would catch, recommended tier
[ ] Findings delivered within 24 hours of receipt
[ ] Outreach Tracker updated to Stage 5 — Findings Sent

STEP 2 — FOLLOW-UP SEQUENCE
[ ] Day 1 (same day as findings): Confirm delivery; invite questions
[ ] Day 2 (no response): First follow-up — "Confirming receipt; any questions?"
[ ] Day 5 (no response): Second follow-up — offer to answer questions by email
    before a call, reference a specific finding from their assessment
[ ] Day 10 (no response): Final follow-up — leave the door open; no pressure
[ ] If they respond at any point: Move to Step 3 immediately

STEP 3 — FOLLOW-UP CALL
[ ] Prospect has expressed interest in discussing further
[ ] Offer a specific time: "I have Thursday at 2pm or Friday at 10am — which works?"
    (Never "when works for you?" — specific options close more efficiently)
[ ] Calendar invite sent with a one-sentence description of what will be covered:
    "20 minutes to walk through your assessment findings and discuss what a build
    would look like for [Company Name]."
[ ] Before the call: Review their specific findings, their industry, their operation size
[ ] On the call: Ask questions first (what's their current process? what's the cost
    of the problem today? what would solving it mean for their team?)
[ ] Present the recommended tier and scope based on THEIR answers
[ ] Quote the price clearly: "[Tier] at [price] CAD + HST. That covers [scope]."
[ ] Ask for the decision: "Does that work for you?" — and then stop talking.

STEP 4 — PROPOSAL
[ ] If they need a written proposal: Deliver it within 24 hours of the call
[ ] Proposal structure (one page maximum):
    - Client name, company, date
    - Problem identified in assessment (2–3 specific findings)
    - Recommended solution (tier + scope)
    - What they receive at handover (source code, model weights, deployment docs, etc.)
    - Timeline (typical 2–4 weeks)
    - Price (one-time, includes all listed items, plus HST)
    - Guarantee (10 hours saved in 30 days or full refund)
    - Next step: Sign and deposit to begin
[ ] Set a follow-up time at end of the call: "I'll send the proposal by tomorrow EOD.
    Can we connect briefly on [specific day] to confirm it covers everything?"

STEP 5 — CLOSE
[ ] Follow up on the proposal within 48 hours if no response
[ ] If they have objections: Handle them directly. Most common are price, scope,
    and "we need to think about it." For each, go back to their specific findings
    and the cost of NOT solving the problem.
[ ] When they agree verbally: "Excellent. I'll get the contract and invoice to you
    today — we can start as soon as the deposit clears."
[ ] Contract and invoice sent within 4 hours of verbal agreement
[ ] Invoice: 50% deposit on start, 50% on delivery (standard structure)
[ ] Outreach Tracker updated to Stage 9 — Closed Won

STEP 6 — POST-CLOSE LEVERAGE
[ ] Deliver the build on time and to scope — this is the entire future of the brand
[ ] At project completion, ask for specific outcome data (hours saved, overcharges
    caught, specific issues found) — this becomes the next case study
[ ] Ask for a written testimonial or brief video (with their permission)
[ ] Ask for referrals: "Is there anyone in your network who might benefit from this?"
[ ] With permission, build the named or anonymized case study for Month 2 content
