HOW THE
TOOL WORKS

This isn't a generic chatbot you throw documents at. It's a private, fine-tuned AI trained on your specific doc formats — blueprints, RFIs, daily logs — built to do one job well: save your project team hours of document review every week.

01

BLUEPRINT AUDITOR

YOUR 300-PAGE
BLUEPRINT,
ACTUALLY READ

You drop in a PDF — could be architectural drawings, structural plans, mechanical/electrical specs, any combination. The AI reads the entire thing, not just the text layer, and returns a structured audit report.

Scope extraction

Pulls out key scope items: floor areas, structural elements, material specs, phasing notes — listed clearly, not buried in page 247.

Conflict detection

Flags when the architectural drawings say one thing and the structural specs say another. The kind of thing that causes $40k change orders if you miss it in bid.

Ambiguity flagging

Identifies vague or underspecified items that need clarification before you mobilize trades. Stops assumptions from becoming delays.

Construction example

"Structural drawing S3.2 specifies W250×89 beams for grid line 4. Architectural A2.1 shows a depth of 200mm at the same location — dimensional conflict requiring RFI."

02

RFI AUDITOR

YOUR RFI LOG,
ACTUALLY MANAGED

Feed it your full RFI backlog — sent, received, answered, unanswered. It reads every one, cross-references against the blueprints, and tells you exactly what's at risk.

Critical path mapping

Which open RFIs are sitting on your critical path? The AI tells you specifically, based on the trade sequence in your specs.

Response status tracking

Identifies which RFIs have been unanswered longest and flags aging items before they become formal delay claims.

Construction example

"RFI #42 (masonry window lintel spec) submitted 18 days ago. No response received. Framing for Level 3 windows cannot proceed without answer. Critical path impact: est. 3–5 days if not resolved by week-end."

03

DAILY LOG SYNTHESIS

WEEKS OF LOGS,
ONE CLEAR REPORT

Your site super files a daily log every day. That's 20+ logs a month per project. Nobody reads all of them. The AI does — and surfaces what matters.

Weekly rollup

Turns 5 days of daily logs into one clean summary: manpower counts, weather delays, deficiency notes, and anything that looks like a pattern.

Delay pattern detection

If the same trade is consistently logging short crew sizes on Mondays, or if a supplier is mentioned in delay notes three weeks running — it connects those dots.

Claims protection

If it ever comes to a dispute, having your delay events systematically documented and cross-referenced is worth a lot. This builds that record automatically.

04

DELAY & CASH-FLOW SIGNALS

WARNINGS BEFORE
THEY'RE EMERGENCIES

Not an accounting system. Not a scheduling tool. Just simple, early signals from your docs: when your draw schedule is at risk, and why.

Delay cluster analysis

When 3+ delay events cluster in the same phase, it flags the potential draw impact before you're having that conversation with the bank.

Risk summary per phase

Each phase of the project gets a simple risk score: low / medium / high, based on what's in your RFIs, logs, and blueprint conflicts.

Logistics tie-in

Predicts delays from port/customs docs that impact material arrival on site — useful if supplier lead times or delivery confirmations are part of your doc set.

// Honest limitations

WHAT THIS IS NOT

✗

Not a full scheduling tool

It reads your schedules and flags risks, but it doesn't replace Procore or your P6/MS Project if you're using those.

✗

Not an accounting system

Cash-flow signals are early warnings from doc patterns, not a real-time ledger or draw management system.

✗

Not an AI chatbot

It doesn't hallucinate answers to vague questions. It reads specific docs and returns structured outputs based on what's actually in them.

✗

Not a cloud SaaS product

No logins. No dashboard. No subscription. It's a local tool that runs on your hardware and produces reports. Built once, runs forever.
