// The four things that cost you the contract renewal

WHERE 3PLS LOSE TRUST

PAIN 01

Customer dashboards lag reality

The customer logs into their portal and sees yesterday's truth. Meanwhile your operations team has been firefighting two diversions and a missed pickup since 6 AM. The trust gap opens the second they email asking why the dashboard hasn't updated.

→ FIX: Carrier Tracker pulls live data from your TMS, EDI, and POD records and pushes a customer-facing view that's actually current.

PAIN 02

Carrier performance is a surprise

A carrier on the customer's lane has been quietly slipping for six weeks. Nobody noticed because the scorecard runs monthly. The customer notices in their own data first, and now the QBR is defensive instead of strategic.

→ FIX: Carrier Tracker flags performance drift the day it starts, with a recommended action — call the rep, swap to backup, or reroute the volume.

PAIN 03

Supplier docs scatter across inboxes

Each customer's suppliers send packing lists, ASNs, and commercial invoices in their own format, half of them wrong. Your ops team spends mornings reconciling — and a missed mismatch becomes a customs hold downstream.

→ FIX: Supplier Intel reads incoming supplier docs, normalizes them, and flags mismatches against the PO before they hit the carrier.

PAIN 04

Invoice audit is a quarterly fire drill

Your customer asks for a freight cost analysis. Now you're matching three months of carrier invoices against quotes by hand. The number you give them is approximate, and the discrepancies you find at month-end are too late to dispute.

→ FIX: Invoice Auditor reads each invoice line against the contracted rate as it lands — discrepancies surface in days, not quarters.
