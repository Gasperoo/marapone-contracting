A regional freight broker recovered $18,400 in their first 30 days with a private AI invoice verification system. Here's exactly what happened.

The business context: mid-sized freight brokerage, processing approximately 340 invoices per month across 6 active carriers. They had a manual invoice review process — an operations team member spot-checking invoices against contracted rates. But "spot-checking" at 340 invoices per month meant full line-level verification wasn't happening consistently. There wasn't enough bandwidth.

What we built: a private invoice verification system trained specifically on their carrier contracts — the actual rate schedules, accessorial charge provisions, fuel surcharge tables, and dimensional weight rules negotiated with each of their 6 carriers. The system processes every invoice against the relevant contract automatically.

Month one findings (47 flagged invoices):

Accessorial charges applied outside contractual scope — carriers billing for services (liftgate, residential delivery, extended area) that contractually required prior authorization or were not applicable to the shipment type. 19 invoices.

Fuel surcharges calculated above the agreed percentage cap — carrier applying the current published rate rather than the contracted capped rate. 17 invoices.

Dimensional weight billed incorrectly — carrier applying dimensional weight calculations to shipments that fell below the contractual threshold for DIM billing. 11 invoices.

Total flagged: $22,100 in potential overcharges. Total recovered after dispute resolution with carriers: $18,400. Timeline: within the first 30 days of deployment.

The follow-up: a review of the prior 14 months of invoices from the same carriers identified an additional $94,000 in overcharges. Partial recovery was pursued through retroactive dispute filings.

The overcharges weren't intentional fraud. They were systematic billing errors that manual spot-checking at volume simply couldn't catch consistently.
